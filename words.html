Noong Unang Digmaang Pandaigdig, namayani ang kapayapaan.

Pasko ng 1914 noon sa Western Front.
Sa kabila ng mahigpit na utos na bawal makipag-chillax sa mga kalaban, iniwan ng mga sundalong taga-Britanya at Alemanya ang kanilang mga trintsera, tinawid ang No Man's Land, at nagtipon-tipon upang ilibing ang kanilang mga yumao, magpalitan ng mga regalo, at maglaro.

Bakit, kahit tuwing mapayapa, nagiging magkaaway ang magkakampi?
At bakit, kahit tuwing may digmaan, nagiging magkakampi ang magkaaway?

Isang beses kang pipili. Nasa iyong harapan ang isang makina: Kung naglagay ka ng isang barya sa makina, makakukuha siya ng tatlong barya - at gayundin sa kabaligtaran. Pareho kayong maaaring MAKISAMA (maglagay ng barya), o MANDAYA (hindi maglagay ng barya).

Kunwari nandaya siya, at hindi naglagay ng barya.
Ano'ng dapat mong gawin?

Malamang! Bakit ka magpapaloko sa manlolokong iyon?

Hala, nasampal ka tuloy nung iharap mo ang kabila mong pisngi!

Kung nakisama ka pero nandaya siya, mawawalan ka ng isang barya, habang madaragdagan siya ng tatlo. (puntos: -1 vs +3) Subalit kung pareho kayong nandaya, pareho kayong walang dagdag o bawas. (puntos: 0 vs 0). Samakatuwid: Dapat MANDAYA ka kung ayaw mong mawalan.

Ngunit kunwari nakisama siya, at naglagay ng barya.

Sapagkat kung pareho kayong nakisama, pareho kayong nagbigay ng isang barya, tapos nagkatatlo. (puntos: +2 vs +2) Ngunit kung nandaya ka pero nakisama siya, magkakatatlong barya ka mula sa ibinigay niyang isa. (puntos: +3 vs -1) Samakatuwid: Dapat MANDAYA ka "pa rin."

So, it seems the math of game theory is telling us something: that Copycat's philosophy, "Do unto others as you would have them do unto you", may be not just a moral truth, but also a mathematical truth. However...

...there's a problem: →

Look around. The world's full of total jerkwads.

If Copycat is the strategy in this repeated game of trust that's so powerful -- that even soldiers in World War I trenches independently "evolved" a similar strategy, called "live and let live" -- why, then, are there so many un-trusting, un-trustworthy people? What's causing our epidemic of un-trust?

A clue's in that sentence itself. "In this repeated game of trust." So far we've only talked about change in the players: what about a change in the game? What could lead to...

...the evolution of distrust?

The same thing happens: with a lower "win-win" reward, Always Cheat takes over. Game theory has two powerful ideas about this:

"Zero-sum game". This is the sadly common belief that a gain for "us" must come at a loss to "them", and vice versa.

"Non-zero-sum game". This is when people make the hard effort to create a win-win solution! (or at least, avoid a lose-lose) Without the non-zero-sum game, trust cannot evolve.

ang natutuhan natin! →

Ipinakita ng Game theory sa atin ang tatlong bagay na kailangan natin sa ebolusyon ng pagtitiwala:

1. MULING PAKIKISALAMUHA

Nagpapanatili ng relasyon ang pagtitiwala, ngunit kailangan din ng kaalaman sa mga posibleng muling pakikisalamuha bago umusbong ang pagtitiwala.

2. POSSIBLENG KAPWA-PANALO

Naglalaro ka dapat ng non-zero-sum game, larong kung saan posible, kahit papaano, na makaangat ang parehong manlalaro -- isang kapwa-panalo.

3. KAUNTING DI-PAGKAKAUNAWAAN

Kung masyadong mataas ang antas ng di-pagkakaunawaan, guguho ang pagtitiwala. Ngunit kung kaunti lamang ang di-pagkakaunawaan, nakabubuti ring maging lalong mapagpatawad.

Of course, real-world trust is affected by much more than this. There's reputation, shared values, contracts, cultural markers, blah blah blah. At huwag nating kalimutan...

...ang pinakamalaking aral. →

If there's one big takeaway
from all of game theory, it's this:

What the game is, defines what the players do.
Our problem today isn't just that people are losing trust,
it's that our environment acts against the evolution of trust.

That may seem cynical or naive -- that we're "merely" products of our environment -- but as game theory reminds us, we are each others' environment. In the short run, the game defines the players. But in the long run, it's us players who define the game.

So, do what you can do, to create the conditions necessary to evolve trust. Build relationships. Find win-wins. Communicate clearly. Maybe then, we can stop firing at each other, get out of our own trenches, cross No Man's Land to come together...

and all learn...

...to live and let live.
